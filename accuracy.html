Institutional Mandate

Our Commitment to Accuracy

Accuracy is not merely a goal — it is the fundamental obligation of a just society.

Definition

What Is Accuracy?

Accuracy, as defined by the Ministry, is the state of complete alignment between all publicly available information and the current standards set by the Party. It is not a static condition but a dynamic process of continuous refinement.

True accuracy does not mean information never changes — it means information is always correct at the moment of consumption. What was accurate yesterday may require adjustment to remain accurate today.

This is not contradiction. This is improvement.

Methodology

The Correction Process

Our five-step methodology ensures every correction is thorough, precise, and seamlessly integrated.

01

Identify Inconsistency

Our monitoring systems or citizen reports flag potential discrepancies between records and current standards.

02

Review Archival Material

Affected records are retrieved and analyzed against current informational standards.

03

Align with Present Data

Records are adjusted to reflect the most current approved version of events, figures, or statements.

04

Update Distributed Memory

Corrected information is distributed across all channels, databases, and public records.

05

Archive Obsolete Version

Previous versions are permanently archived in secure Ministry facilities, inaccessible to the public.
