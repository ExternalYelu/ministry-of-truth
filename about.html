Who We Are

Institutional Overview

Founded in 1984, the Ministry of Truth (Minitrue) serves as Oceania's central authority for informational integrity. Our mandate is the preservation, refinement, and distribution of all approved knowledge.

We employ over 42,000 dedicated professionals across four specialized departments, each working tirelessly to ensure that public records, news, education, and entertainment reflect the most current standards of accuracy.

Our work is not merely administrative — it is essential to the stability and unity of our great society. Every correction we make, every record we align, strengthens the foundation upon which Oceania stands.

Founding Principles

The Pillars of Our Mission

Every action taken by the Ministry is guided by three immutable principles, established at our founding and upheld without exception.

01

Protect the Narrative

A unified narrative is the bedrock of social order. Contradictions, however minor, threaten the stability of the collective understanding. It is our duty to identify and resolve such discrepancies before they propagate.

02

Maintain Alignment

Every citizen deserves access to the same truth. Discrepancies between personal memory and the official record are not the fault of the individual — they are inefficiencies we are here to correct.

03

Serve With Vigilance

Misinformation is not a passive threat — it is an active force that must be countered continuously. Our work never stops, because the enemies of truth never rest.

Our Structure

Organizational Departments

The Ministry operates through four highly specialized departments, each critical to our mission of safeguarding informational integrity.

The Minister

Supreme authority over all informational matters. Appointed directly by the Party. All departmental directives flow from this office.

Department of Records

Manages the correction and alignment of all historical documents, ensuring consistency with current standards.

3,200 analysts process over 10,000 corrections daily across all archival systems.

Dept. of Historical Integrity

Ensures that all historical narratives reflect the approved interpretation of events past and present.

Maintains a living archive of 14 million records, updated in real time.

Dept. of Statistical Enhancement

Optimizes all numerical data, production figures, and metrics to reflect improvements and continued progress.

Every published figure passes through three tiers of enhancement before release.

Dept. of Public Sentiment

Monitors and guides public perception, ensuring alignment between citizen attitudes and Party objectives.

Operates sentiment-tracking networks across all 12 Oceanian sectors.

Our Journey

Timeline of Progress

Key milestones in our ongoing mission to safeguard informational integrity across Oceania.

1984

Ministry Established

The Ministry of Truth is formally established as the central authority for informational integrity across all of Oceania. Big Brother declares informational alignment the highest civic duty.

1990

Full Archival Digitization

Complete digitization of all pre-Revolution records, enabling more efficient correction and alignment processes. Physical archives are sealed permanently.

2002

Predictive Narrative Framework

Introduction of predictive narrative alignment technology, allowing corrections to be implemented before inconsistencies become public. A landmark in proactive truth management.

2010

Citizen Participation Initiative

Launch of the public reporting system, empowering citizens to identify and report informational discrepancies. Over 1.2 million reports submitted in the first year alone.

2020

Global Alignment Protocol

Implementation of the Global Alignment Protocol, synchronizing informational standards across all Oceanian territories. Cross-border narrative discrepancies eliminated.

2025

Real-Time Monitoring

Deployment of real-time narrative monitoring across all public channels, achieving 99.97% consistency. The remaining 0.03% is under active investigation.

2026

Accuracy Reform Act

Landmark legislation granting the Ministry expanded authority to proactively correct emerging informational discrepancies. A new era of truth begins.
